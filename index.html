The Vision

Transforming a day into a week of movement.

Juneteenth is more than a date, it is a celebration of resilience. While there will be no Nationally led Juneteenth celebration, there canbe nationwide celebrations through our local chapters. This guide empowers your local chapter to host a gathering that isn't just a "party," but a moment of true understanding and community.

Yesterday's Legacy

Honoring the ancestors, educating our teams on June 19, 1865, and acknowledging the triumph of liberation.

Today's Promise

Spotlighting Black excellence within our firm today and supporting local Black-owned businesses.

Tomorrow's Hope

Looking forward. Commitments to build a legacy of equity and freedom for future generations.

Spread the Joy: A Week of Celebration

Don't limit the celebration to just one day! We encourage chapters to stretch the festivities throughout the week! Whether you are planning one, or many events, get creative for the day you choose and truly make this a week of celebration

  • • Monday: "Yesterday's Legacy" educational outreach.
  • • Tuesday: Cultural Playlist and virtual background share.
  • • Wednesday: Mid-week Trivia or Black-owned catering.
  • • Thursday: Main Event! Block party or office mixer.

* Note: Don't settle on Thursday! You can host the main event on any day that works best for your chapter.

The Freedom Friday Nudge

"Chapter Leaders: We suggest nudging your office leadership to support a flexible schedule. Encourage teams to put in extra time Monday-Thursday so they can fully disconnect and celebrate on Friday."

A gift of time is the best way to honor freedom.

Why the Focus on Red?

Red symbolizes the resilience and strength of enslaved ancestors. In West African traditions, red Kola nuts and Hibiscus flowers were offered as signs of hospitality and celebration.

The Libation Station

"Pouring out honors to the past."

Zero-Proof / Non-Alcoholic

Traditional Hibiscus "Red Drink"

The ultimate Juneteenth staple. Sweet, tart, and deeply refreshing.

Recipe idea: Dried hibiscus flowers, steeped with ginger, cloves, and cane sugar. Serve over ice with a lime wheel.

Jubilee Watermelon Lemonade

A visually stunning red beverage perfect for summer office parties.

Recipe idea: Freshly blended watermelon juice mixed with tart lemonade. Garnish with mint sprigs.
Contains Alcohol (21+)

The 1865 Bourbon Smash

A nod to the history of Black mixologists in the American South.

Recipe idea: Bourbon (ideally from a Black-owned distillery), muddled blackberries, fresh lemon juice, and simple syrup.

Liberation Sangria

A celebratory, batch-friendly red drink for larger gatherings.

Recipe idea: Red wine, brandy, chopped red apples, strawberries, and a splash of sparkling water.

Safety First: Ensure alcohol is served at a clearly labeled, separate station with non-alcoholic options equally represented.
